Neurodiverse

Absence of the Corpus Callosum (ACC) is a congenital brain defect and is when the structure that connects the two hemispheres of the brain, is missing. The effects of the disorder range from subtle or mild to severe and the condition can cause cognitive impairment and developmental delays. A child with ACC may experience some language and speech delays as well as delays in the development of motor skills. Many people with ACC have normal intelligence and can survive with the condition.
